Canny is genuinely good software. It's clean, it works, and it solves a real problem. If you're reading this, you probably already know that.

The issue isn't the product. It's the moment you open your invoice and realize you're paying $275 a month because your product grew past 1,000 users. That's the thing about per-user pricing it punishes you for succeeding.

We built OpenFeed because we kept hitting that wall. This post isn't a hit piece on Canny. It's an honest comparison to help you decide what actually makes sense for your situation.

## What Canny does well

Before anything else  Canny has earned its reputation. The feedback board is polished, the deduplication is solid, and their Autopilot AI feature genuinely saves time if you're getting feedback from multiple sources like Zendesk and Intercom.

If you're at a Series B company with a dedicated product ops team and $300/month feels like a rounding error, Canny is probably fine for you. Stop reading and go back to work.

This post is for everyone else.

## Where things break down

**The pricing scales with your user base, not your usage**

Canny's model is based on "tracked users" anyone who votes, comments, or submits feedback. Hit 1,000 users? You're on the $79/month plan minimum. Hit 2,500? You're looking at $159/month. These aren't enterprise companies. These are indie hackers and early-stage startups who are still figuring out if their product has legs.

**AI features cost extra on top**

Canny's Autopilot is genuinely useful, but it runs on a credit system. Credits cost $0.10 each, don't roll over month to month, and a credit card is required just to keep it running. Connecting it to Intercom or Zendesk uses credits per conversation processed. It adds up fast and unpredictably.

**Your branding, their logo**

On lower plans, Canny puts their "Powered by Canny" badge on your product. Your users see it. Removing it costs more. It's a small thing, but it matters when you care about your product feeling cohesive.

**No self-hosting option**

If you want full control over your data, Canny isn't an option. Everything goes through their infrastructure.

## What OpenFeed does differently

OpenFeed is open source, MIT licensed, and built around a different set of principles.

**Flat pricing, no tracked users**

You pay a flat monthly fee regardless of how many users interact with your widget. A thousand users or a hundred thousand the price doesn't move. We made this decision early and we're not changing it.

**AI is included, no credits**

The AI Product Advisor is available on every paid plan. No credit system, no overages, no surprises at the end of the month. Ask it what to build next and it'll query your actual feedback data, check your roadmap, and give you a specific answer. It's agentic it investigates before it responds.

**No branding on any paid plan**

Every paid plan including the cheapest one removes OpenFeed branding entirely. Your product should look like your product.

**Self-host for free**

Clone the repo, run one command, and you have a fully working OpenFeed instance on your own server. Docker handles everything. Your data never leaves your infrastructure.

## When Canny is still the right choice

If you need deep integrations with Salesforce, HubSpot, or enterprise SSO Canny has built these out properly and OpenFeed hasn't yet. If your feedback workflow depends heavily on pulling from Intercom or Zendesk conversations automatically, Canny's Autopilot does this better than anything we currently offer.

Large teams with complex permission requirements and dedicated customer success managers will probably find Canny's enterprise tier worth the price.

## When OpenFeed makes more sense

If you're an indie hacker, a small startup, or a bootstrapped SaaS OpenFeed is built for you. Flat pricing means you know exactly what you're paying every month. The one-line widget install means you're up and running in five minutes. And if you ever want to move off cloud hosting, your data is yours to take.
